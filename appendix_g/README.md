## Appendix G — LDA Stop-Word Lists (LDA Topic Labelling)

This appendix supports the LDA Topic Labelling section by listing in full the two stop-word lists applied before LDA topic labelling. Both lists are used in the title-LDA pipeline (Title-LDA Labelling Procedure) and the full-text-LDA pipeline (Title Input versus Full-Text Input); the step numbers below refer to the eight-step full-text cleaning sequence tabulated in the Title Input versus Full-Text Input section. Words are listed alphabetically.

### G.1 Legal stop-word list (51 terms)

A hand-curated list of structural legal vocabulary — words that recur across Act titles and statutory text regardless of subject — defined as `LEGAL_STOPWORDS` in `src/network_analysis/community.py` and removed at Step 4 of the cleaning sequence. It targets the procedural and structural terms most common in NZ legislation drafting, so that the LDA topic is driven by subject vocabulary rather than by drafting scaffolding. The list began as 37 terms; twenty procedural drafting words (*shall*, *subsection*, *amended*, *principal*, *thereof*, …) were added after the full-text-LDA analysis of the Title Input versus Full-Text Input section found them dominating community topics — these are near-absent from Act titles, so the addition strips full-text procedural noise while leaving the title-LDA labels essentially unchanged. Six terms — *law*, *laws*, *public*, *crown*, *local*, and *maori* — were subsequently removed because each is a variant of a parliamentary-committee subject group (`{law, laws}`, `{public, publics}`, `{crown, crowns}`, `{local, locals, locally}`, `{maori, māori}`); excluding every committee-subject term keeps the stop-word filter from removing the vocabulary the content-similarity recall is scored against. The list therefore stands at 51 terms.

### G.3 Keyword-depth sensitivity

The title-LDA representation uses the top-15 keywords per community (LDA Representation Depth). This depth was confirmed by recomputing the per-community Jaccard for the community that best matches the Finance and Expenditure committee as the keyword set widens from 10 to 40. The LDA topic ranks the full vocabulary once, so each depth is a longer prefix of the same ranking. The intersection — the variants of every committee group a keyword hits — saturates at 10 by a depth of 15, because every matchable subject word already sits in the top ranks; the union — the committee's variants together with the keywords — keeps growing. The Jaccard therefore peaks at 15 and declines monotonically as the depth increases, while the best-matching committee stays Finance throughout, so the decline reflects denominator growth rather than a change of subject.
